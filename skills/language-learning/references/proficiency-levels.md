# Proficiency Level Guidelines

## A1 (Beginner)

### Focus Areas
- Basic greetings and introductions
- Present tense only
- Simple subject-verb-object sentences
- Numbers, days, colors
- Essential vocabulary (food, family, common objects)

### Grammar Complexity
- Only present tense
- Simple declarative sentences
- Basic question forms (what, where, when)

### Example Cards

**Vocabulary:**
- "el gato" → "the cat"
- "Ich bin" → "I am"

**Simple Sentences:**
- "Yo {{c1::tengo}} un perro." → "I have a dog."
- "Das ist {{c1::gut}}." → "That is good."

### Sentence Length
Maximum 5-7 words

## A2 (Elementary)

### Focus Areas
- Past tense introduction (simple past)
- Basic future expressions
- Common irregular verbs
- Everyday situations (shopping, directions)
- Connecting words (and, but, because)

### Grammar Complexity
- Present and simple past
- Basic future ("going to" constructions)
- Simple conjunctions

### Example Cards

**Common Phrases:**
- "¿Puedes {{c1::ayudarme}}?" → "Can you help me?"
- "Je {{c1::suis allé}} au marché." → "I went to the market."

**Past Tense:**
- "Ayer {{c1::comí}} pizza." → "Yesterday I ate pizza."

### Sentence Length
Maximum 8-10 words

## B1 (Intermediate)

### Focus Areas
- Multiple past tenses
- Conditional mood introduction
- Idiomatic expressions
- Describing experiences and events
- Expressing opinions
- Common phrasal verbs

### Grammar Complexity
- Present, past (simple & continuous), future
- Basic conditionals ("would," "could")
- Subordinate clauses
- Modal verbs

### Example Cards

**Idiomatic Expressions:**
- "Me {{c1::da igual}} si llueve." → "I don't care if it rains."
- "Das {{c1::kommt darauf an}}." → "That depends."

**Complex Structures:**
- "Si tuviera tiempo, {{c1::iría}} al cine." → "If I had time, I would go to the cinema."

### Sentence Length
Up to 12-15 words

## B2 (Upper Intermediate)

### Focus Areas
- Subjunctive mood
- Nuanced vocabulary
- Abstract concepts
- Complex time expressions
- Passive voice
- Advanced phrasal verbs and collocations

### Grammar Complexity
- All major tenses including subjunctive
- Passive constructions
- Complex conditionals
- Reported speech

### Example Cards

**Subjunctive:**
- "Es importante que {{c1::sepas}} la verdad." → "It's important that you know the truth."
- "Je doute qu'il {{c1::vienne}}." → "I doubt he will come."

**Abstract Concepts:**
- "La decisión {{c1::conllevó}} graves consecuencias." → "The decision entailed serious consequences."

### Sentence Length
Up to 15-18 words

## C1 (Advanced)

### Focus Areas
- Sophisticated vocabulary
- Literary and formal registers
- Complex grammatical structures
- Subtle distinctions in meaning
- Stylistic devices
- Regional variations

### Grammar Complexity
- All grammatical structures
- Emphasis on subtle distinctions
- Complex embedded clauses
- Advanced aspect and mood usage

### Example Cards

**Nuanced Vocabulary:**
- "El argumento {{c1::esgrimido}} resultó convincente." → "The argument put forward proved convincing."
- "Die {{c1::vielschichtige}} Problematik..." → "The multifaceted problem..."

**Subtle Distinctions:**
- "Il {{c1::aurait dû}} le faire." → "He should have done it." (past conditional)

### Sentence Length
Up to 20 words acceptable

## C2 (Mastery)

### Focus Areas
- Native-like expression
- Cultural references
- Sophisticated humor and wordplay
- Professional/academic language
- Historical or archaic forms (if relevant)
- Fine semantic distinctions

### Grammar Complexity
- Flawless command of all structures
- Focus on stylistic variation
- Context-appropriate register

### Example Cards

**Sophisticated Expression:**
- "No es tanto que lo {{c1::desdeñe}}, sino que prefiere alternativas más viables." → "It's not so much that he disdains it, but rather he prefers more viable alternatives."

**Cultural References:**
- "Hacer algo {{c1::a la chita callando}}" → "To do something on the sly" (Spanish idiom)

### Sentence Length
Natural length for the expression (can exceed 20 words if appropriate)

## General Guidelines

### Adjusting Difficulty
- **Too easy**: User breezes through without thinking
- **Too hard**: User frequently fails cards or takes >10 seconds
- **Just right**: User pauses briefly (2-5 seconds) before recalling

### Progressive Learning
- Start conservative (estimate one level below)
- Gradually introduce next-level structures
- Mix difficulty within appropriate range
- User feedback is crucial for calibration

### Red Flags by Level
- **A1/A2**: Subjunctive, literary terms, idioms
- **B1/B2**: Archaic forms, very formal register
- **C1/C2**: Overly simple vocabulary, basic grammar focus
