# Card Examples

## Good Examples

### Cloze Cards

**Example 1: Common Verb (Catalan)**
- Target Language: "He {{c1::trencat}} el got sense voler."
- Source Language: "I broke the glass by accident."
- Description: "trencar (to break) - common irregular verb"

**Example 2: Idiomatic Expression (Spanish)**
- Target Language: "Me {{c1::da igual}} lo que pienses."
- Source Language: "I don't care what you think."
- Description: "dar igual - idiomatic expression for indifference"

**Example 3: Gender Marker (German)**
- Target Language: "{{c1::Der}} Hund ist groß."
- Source Language: "The dog is big."
- Description: "der (masculine article) with Hund"

**Example 4: Phrasal Verb (English)**
- Target Language: "I need to {{c1::look into}} this problem."
- Source Language: "Necesito investigar este problema."
- Description: "look into - phrasal verb meaning to investigate"

### Basic Front/Back Cards

**Example 1: Simple Vocabulary**
- Front: "to wander alone"
- Back: "deambular sol"
- Word Type: "verb"

**Example 2: Common Phrase**
- Front: "por supuesto"
- Back: "of course"
- Notes: "Very common in casual conversation"

**Example 3: Adjective with Gender**
- Front: "happy (masculine)"
- Back: "el content"
- Notes: "Catalan - feminine: la contenta"

## Bad Examples (What to Avoid)

### Too Complex

**Bad:**
- Target: "Si hagués {{c1::tingut}} més temps, hauria {{c2::pogut}} {{c3::acabar}} el projecte abans de la reunió d'ahir."
- **Problem**: Multiple cloze deletions, complex grammar, too long

**Better:**
- Split into 3 separate cards:
  1. "Si hagués {{c1::tingut}} més temps..." (past subjunctive of tenir)
  2. "Hauria {{c1::pogut}} fer-ho." (conditional of poder)
  3. "Vaig {{c1::acabar}} el projecte." (preterite of acabar)

### Too Formal/Literary

**Bad:**
- Target: "El monarca {{c1::proclamó}} su soberanía ante la asamblea."
- **Problem**: Overly formal vocabulary rarely used in conversation

**Better:**
- Target: "El rey {{c1::anunció}} su decisión."
- More natural and useful vocabulary

### Ambiguous Cloze

**Bad:**
- Target: "Elle {{c1::est}} allée au {{c2::marché}}."
- **Problem**: Testing grammar and vocabulary simultaneously

**Better:**
- Create 2 cards:
  1. "Elle {{c1::est}} allée au marché." (auxiliary verb)
  2. "Elle est allée au {{c1::marché}}." (vocabulary)

### Missing Context

**Bad:**
- Front: "sein"
- Back: "to be"
- **Problem**: No context for irregular forms, usage, or register

**Better:**
- Target: "Ich {{c1::bin}} müde."
- Source: "I am tired."
- Description: "sein (to be) - first person singular present"

## Note Type Selection

### Use Cloze When:
- Teaching words in context
- Focusing on a specific grammatical structure
- Showing idiomatic usage
- Demonstrating word order

### Use Basic Front/Back When:
- Introducing new vocabulary without context
- Testing recognition of phrases or expressions
- Quick vocabulary drills
- Gender/article memorization

### Use Reverse Cards When:
- Building active vocabulary (production, not just recognition)
- Learning synonyms
- Practicing translation in both directions
